A Travel Guide for Italy

Posted by Start Bootstrap on June 2nd, 2023

As a familiar top travel destination, Italy boasts itself to having it all. From the beautiful coastline of Amalfi, to the ancient history of Rome, to the fashion empire of Milan, you will not find yourself bored here.

And not to mention, be prepared to experience the best food on the entire planet.

While my study abroad didn't allow for a full Italian tour, we were still able to experience some amazing places including Venice, Positano, and Florence.

I can tell you after experiencing just that, your trip to Italy will be worth it. The cuisine is impeccable. The people are impossible to not love. The culture is exquisite.

Venice

Rumors have it the city of Venice is on it's way downwards, literally, so if you're at all interested in visiting, make it sooner over later.

Venice was a pleasant surprise! It truly was unlike anything I'd ever seen -- a city quite literally on water. The canals were charming, and felt straight out of a movie.

Make sure you bring around 50 euros for a gondola ride - it's a must! Seeing the city from the water canals really puts into perspective how unique of a city it is.

Positano

As hyped up as the Amalfi Coast seems to be, it truly deserves it all. Make sure you get a view of the coast from the water, wether that be a ferry, public excursion boat, or private. The colorful buildings dotting the chiseled Italian cliffs is truly a sight to see.

My view from the ferry approaching Positano

If you're wanting to enjoy the private beach, be prepared to drop a $40 chair and service fee. Otherwise, there's a small section of the beach set aside for public domain.

Make sure you set aside time exploring the city and it's charming towns. Get ready to sweat, it's quite a workout as it's all uphill! Make sure to stop by the iconic CB stores that showcase handmade floral dresses and bags.
